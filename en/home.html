Welcome!

The properties of nanostructures and nanoparticles are a fundamental part of nanoscience and nanotechnology, as most nano-devices will be made up of these tiny objects. Materials that we traditionally do not value, exhibit very different characteristics from what would be expected when considering a macroscopic object made of the same material at the Nano scale. Controlling the properties of these structures plays an important role because of the potential to change their behavior according to the desired need or application. The Group of Nanostructures and Applied Physics at UNAL La Paz considers the enormous transformative potential in areas such as water quality, energy generation/recovery, use of NPK sensors for the agricultural sector, hard coatings on cutting tools, among others. With an approach characterized by the interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary nature of its members, the objective is to close the gaps that exist between rural and urban areas of the Colombian Caribbean in terms of nanoscience, nanotechnology and converging technologies.

Examples of our work

SISMA is a system for monitoring physical and chemical parameters that allows monitoring temperature, humidity and atmospheric pressure, as well as soil humidity and temperature remotely. This makes use of IoT, which has a strong relationship with the use of information through Industry 4.0 and Big Data.



SISMA is a low-cost, easy-to-use and modular prototype that has the ability to measure up to 5 parameters and send information remotely via bluetooth or wifi.
